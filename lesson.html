Habari — "How are you"

One of the first signs taught to new learners. Shown here with the standard form and two regional variants used in Nairobi and the coast.

Handshape

Open palm, fingers relaxed and together

Movement

Short outward tilt from the chest, twice

Context

Informal greeting, any time of day

Habari is typically the first exchange in a KSL conversation. The sign starts at chest height and moves outward with a slight tilt of the wrist, repeated once for emphasis. It can be paired with a raised eyebrow for a more informal, familiar tone.

Coastal signers often soften the motion into a single, slower tilt, while Nairobi signers tend to repeat it twice in quick succession. Both are considered standard KSL and are understood everywhere.

Habari — How are you

How it is signed

Habari starts at chest height and moves outward with a slight tilt of the wrist, repeated once for emphasis. It can be paired with a raised eyebrow for a more informal, familiar tone.

Coastal signers often soften the motion into a single, slower tilt, while Nairobi signers tend to repeat it twice in quick succession. Both are considered standard KSL and are understood everywhere.

A Nairobi variant
Quick double tilt, crisp, used in schools.
B Coastal variant
Single slow tilt, softer, often with smile.
